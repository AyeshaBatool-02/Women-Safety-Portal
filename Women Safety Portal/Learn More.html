Women Rights and Laws in Pakistan

Introduction

In Pakistan, women are protected under various laws and have specific rights that ensure their safety and equality. These laws cover various aspects of life including marriage, employment, education, and protection against violence.

Key Rights and Laws

  • Constitutional Rights: The Constitution of Pakistan guarantees equality before the law and prohibits discrimination on the basis of sex (Article 25).
  • Protection Against Harassment: The Protection Against Harassment of Women at the Workplace Act 2010 aims to create a safe working environment for women.
  • Domestic Violence: The Domestic Violence (Prevention and Protection) Act 2012 provides protection to women against domestic abuse.
  • Inheritance Rights: Under Islamic law, women have the right to inherit property, which is also recognized by Pakistani law.
  • Marriage and Divorce: The Muslim Family Laws Ordinance 1961 regulates marriage and divorce, ensuring the protection of women's rights in marital relationships.
  • Child Marriage Restraint: The Child Marriage Restraint Act 1929 prohibits marriage below the age of 18 for girls.
  • Acid Control and Acid Crime Prevention: The Acid Control and Acid Crime Prevention Act 2011 imposes strict penalties on perpetrators of acid attacks.

Recent Developments

Recent legislative measures have further strengthened the protection of women's rights in Pakistan. Initiatives such as the Punjab Protection of Women Against Violence Act 2016 highlight the ongoing efforts to address violence against women comprehensively.

Support and Resources

Several NGOs and government bodies are working to support women's rights in Pakistan. Organizations like the Aurat Foundation, War Against Rape (WAR), and the National Commission on the Status of Women (NCSW) provide resources, legal aid, and advocacy for women's rights.
